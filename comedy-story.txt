The Day My Code Finally Worked

Once upon a time, there was a developer named Bob who spent three days debugging a function that wouldn't work. He tried everything: Stack Overflow, rubber duck debugging, and even talking to his houseplant named "Console Logger."

On the third day, exhausted and desperate, Bob decided to add a simple console.log() statement. To his amazement, the code suddenly worked perfectly. Confused, he removed the console.log(). The code broke again. He added it back. It worked. Removed it. Broke. Added it. Worked.

Bob stared at his screen for a full minute before realizing the horrible truth: he had been missing a semicolon two lines above, and the act of adding the console.log() forced him to properly format his code.

The houseplant had no comment.

Bob decided that Console Logger deserved a promotion to Senior Debugging Officer. The plant now sits in a bigger pot with better soil.

The moral of the story? Sometimes the bug isn't in your code—it's in your confidence. Also, water your plants; they're working harder than you think.

THE END

P.S. - Bob later discovered that JavaScript doesn't actually require semicolons most of the time. He had a good cry about that.
